Project 02  ·  Architecture & Design

Knowledge Architecture Frameworks

Three interlocking frameworks that form the intellectual architecture of enterprise AI content — defining how knowledge is classified, structured, and assessed for AI retrievability.

This is the design layer that sits between raw content and AI outputs: the taxonomies, schemas, and evaluation criteria that determine what AI systems know and how reliably they retrieve it.

  • JTBD registry spans three AI product families — the canonical source of truth for content architecture decisions
  • GEO framework evaluates content retrievability across external AI engines and internal RAG systems
  • Knowledge taxonomy classifies content by cognitive function — not just topic — enabling precision retrieval
  • All three frameworks are actively used by content and architecture teams across the organisation
